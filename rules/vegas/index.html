Vegas

2 v 2 — team digits duel, birdies flip the number

The basics

Vegas is a 2-v-2 game where each team's two scores combine into a single number, low digit first: a 4 and a 5 make 45. Low team number wins the difference in points — 45 against 62 pays 17. Every player on the winning side books the full difference.

Scores above 9 count as 9, which keeps a blow-up hole from creating a three-digit disaster.

The flip

Make a birdie and your opponents' number flips high digit first — their 4-and-8 becomes 84 instead of 48. It's the signature Vegas moment. If both teams birdie, no flip.

The flip means no lead is safe and a single putt can swing a hole by fifty points.

Options
