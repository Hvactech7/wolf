Wolf

First off the tee hunts with a partner — or goes lone wolf

The basics

Wolf is a four-player points game built around one dramatic decision per hole: who hunts with whom.

The first player to tee off is the Wolf. The tee order rotates every hole, so everyone takes an equal number of turns. On the last two holes, many groups (and Battle Tees, as a house option) give the Wolf role to whoever is last in points — a built-in comeback mechanic.

Picking a partner — one shot at each

The Wolf watches the second player hit. Like the drive? Claim them as a partner. Pass, and the third player hits — but the second player is now off the table, no going back. Same choice on the third: take them, or pass and the fourth player hits — and the Wolf is stuck with whatever that drive looks like.

Best ball of each side decides the hole. Ties push — with an optional carryover that doubles the next decided hole.

Going alone

Lone Wolf: after hitting a drive they love, the Wolf can declare against all three. Bigger points if they win, and every field player collects if the field takes the hole.

Blind Lone Wolf: declared before hitting a single shot — the biggest gamble in the game, at the biggest payout.

In Battle Tees

Every point value is adjustable in setup — partner wins, lone wins, blind wins, and what the field earns for taking the Wolf down. Closest-to-pin on par 3s is worth a bonus point (ball must be on the green).

The app tracks the rotation, the picks, the carryovers, and the running points — and the Settle screen turns points into exact payouts.
