Nassau

The classic — front nine, back nine, overall

The basics

The most famous golf bet in America is three bets in one round: the front nine, the back nine, and the overall 18 — each its own match. A “$5 Nassau” means $5 on each.

It's match play — holes won, not strokes — played 1 v 1 or 2 v 2 best ball. A rough front nine can't touch the back-nine bet, so everyone grinds to the last putt.

The press

A side that's down can press: a brand-new bet at the same stake covering the remaining holes of that nine. Down 2 with four to play? Press, and there's a fourth bet running.

Battle Tees supports presses anytime you're down, classic 2-down automatics (every bet spawns a press the first time it falls exactly two down — cascades and all), or off. The app tracks every live bet so nobody's doing press math in the cart.

The name

Born at Nassau Country Club in Glen Cove, New York, around 1900 — invented, the story goes, so badly beaten members never lost worse than “3 points” in the posted results. The county — and the Bahamian capital — are both named for the same House of Orange-Nassau.
